The Tubelox Show Build

Why I built it the way I did

This Tubelox build wasn’t designed to be flashy or complicated. It was designed to be observed.

When I build enrichment for my cats, especially in a multi-cat household, I try not to force outcomes. I don’t build what I think they should use. I build structures that allow them to show me what they choose.

This particular build was created as a “show build” — a calm, open structure where access points, height, and flow mattered more than aesthetics alone. I wanted to see how each cat approached it, how long they stayed, and whether it became part of their routine rather than a novelty.

One of the things I appreciate about Tubelox is the flexibility. I can adjust layouts without tearing everything down, which is important when you’re working with animals who communicate through behavior rather than words.

In this video, you’ll see moments of hesitation, curiosity, confidence, and quiet use. Those moments matter to me more than instant excitement. Calm engagement is the goal.

This build is part of a larger series where I let the cats lead, and I simply document what happens when the environment supports regulation instead of overstimulation.
